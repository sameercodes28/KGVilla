## Combined Room Naming Pattern

Swedish floor plans often show open-plan areas using "/" between names:

```
ROOM1 / ROOM2
```

Examples:
- `KÖK/MATPLATS` = Open kitchen with dining
- `GROVENTRÉ/TVÄTT` = Utility entrance with laundry
- `WC/BAD` = Toilet with bathroom
- `MATPLATS/VARDAGSRUM` = Dining opening to living room

## Reading Area Measurements

Areas are shown in square meters (m²):

| Format | Example |
|--------|---------|
| After room name | SOVRUM 1 12.5 m² |
| Below room name | SOVRUM 1<br>12.5 m² |
| With comma (Swedish) | 12,5 m² |
| With period | 12.5 m² |

## Tips for Reading Floor Plans

1. **Look for m² symbols** - Areas are usually shown near room names
2. **Check the title block** - Often shows BOYTA (living area) total
3. **Note the "/" pattern** - Indicates combined/open rooms
4. **Equipment labels are small** - VP, TM, TT show appliance locations
5. **Fire ratings (EI30)** - Usually shown on garage walls
