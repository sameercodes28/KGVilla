# 1. INTRODUCTION

## 1.1 Purpose of This Document

This encyclopedia serves as the definitive reference for Swedish residential construction regulations as implemented in the KGVilla quantity take-off system. It provides construction professionals, developers, and AI systems with comprehensive knowledge about:

- Legal requirements and their practical implications
- Technical specifications and tolerances
- Cost implications of compliance
- Cross-references between related standards
- Regional variations and exceptions

## 1.2 Regulatory Hierarchy in Sweden

Understanding the Swedish regulatory hierarchy is essential for compliance:

```
LEVEL 1: LAWS (Lagar)
├── Plan- och Bygglagen (PBL) - The Planning and Building Act
├── Miljöbalken - The Environmental Code
└── Arbetsmiljölagen - Work Environment Act

LEVEL 2: ORDINANCES (Förordningar)
├── Plan- och Byggförordningen (PBF)
└── Miljöprövningsförordningen

LEVEL 3: REGULATIONS (Föreskrifter)
├── BBR - Boverkets Byggregler (Mandatory)
├── EKS - Boverkets konstruktionsregler (Mandatory)
├── ELSÄK-FS - Electrical safety regulations (Mandatory)
└── OVK requirements (Mandatory)

LEVEL 4: GENERAL GUIDELINES (Allmänna Råd)
├── Boverket's general guidelines
└── Agency recommendations

LEVEL 5: INDUSTRY STANDARDS (Branschregler)
├── Säker Vatten - Plumbing (Required for insurance)
├── BBV - Ceramic tiles (Required for warranty)
├── GVK - Flooring (Required for warranty)
├── AMA Hus - Reference specifications (Contractual)
└── ABT 06 - Contract terms (Contractual)
```

# 2. BBR 2025 - BOVERKETS BYGGREGLER

## 2.1 Overview

**Full Name:** Boverkets Byggregler
**Current Version:** BBR 29 (BFS 2011:6 with amendments through BFS 2024:x)
**Effective Date:** July 1, 2025
**Authority:** Boverket (Swedish National Board of Housing, Building and Planning)
**Legal Basis:** Plan- och Bygglagen (PBL) Chapter 8
**Enforcement:** Mandatory for all new construction and major renovations

### 2.1.1 Historical Context

BBR has evolved significantly since its first publication:

| Version | Year | Key Changes |
|---------|------|-------------|
| BBR 1-5 | 1994-1998 | Initial framework established |
| BBR 6-10 | 1999-2002 | Energy requirements introduced |
| BBR 11-15 | 2003-2006 | Fire safety modernized |
| BBR 16-20 | 2007-2010 | Accessibility requirements strengthened |
| BBR 21-25 | 2011-2018 | Energy efficiency tightened |
| BBR 26-28 | 2019-2023 | Climate focus introduced |
| BBR 29 | 2025 | Climate declaration mandatory |

### 2.1.2 Structure of BBR

BBR is organized into 9 chapters:

- **Chapter 1:** Administrative provisions
- **Chapter 2:** Safe use
- **Chapter 3:** Accessibility and usability (Tillgänglighet)
- **Chapter 4:** (Reserved)
- **Chapter 5:** Fire safety (Brandskydd)
- **Chapter 6:** Hygiene, health, and environment (Hygien, hälsa och miljö)
- **Chapter 7:** (Reserved)
- **Chapter 8:** Safety in use (Säkerhet vid användning)
- **Chapter 9:** Energy management (Energihushållning)

## 2.2 BBR Chapter 3: Accessibility (Tillgänglighet)

### 2.2.1 General Principles (BBR 3:1)

Swedish accessibility requirements are among the world's most comprehensive. The philosophy is **"Design for all"** - buildings should be usable by people with varying abilities from day one.

**Key Principle:** A person using a wheelchair must be able to:
- Enter the building independently
- Access all primary functions on the entrance floor
- Use at least one bathroom
- Move through all corridors and doorways

### 2.2.2 Entrance Requirements (BBR 3:142)

#### External Approach
| Requirement | Specification | Rationale |
|-------------|---------------|-----------|
| Path width | ≥ 1.30 m | Wheelchair + companion |
| Max gradient | 1:12 (8.3%) | Independent wheelchair access |
| Cross-slope | ≤ 2% | Prevents wheelchair drift |
| Surface | Firm, slip-resistant | Year-round accessibility |
| Level change | ≤ 20 mm at door | Threshold accessibility |

#### Main Entrance Door
| Requirement | Specification | Notes |
|-------------|---------------|-------|
| Clear width | ≥ 0.80 m | After door open 90° |
| Threshold height | ≤ 20 mm | Can be 25mm if chamfered |
| Handle height | 0.80 - 1.10 m | One-hand operable |
| Operating force | ≤ 30 N | For manual doors |
| Floor space inside | 1.30 m turning circle | After entry |

**Cost Impact:** An accessible entrance typically adds 5,000-15,000 SEK vs. a standard entrance due to:
- Wider door frame (Module 10 vs Module 9)
- Low-threshold door sill
- Accessible hardware
- Ramped approach if grade difference exists

### 2.2.3 Interior Circulation (BBR 3:143)

#### Corridors
| Requirement | Specification | Purpose |
|-------------|---------------|---------|
| Minimum width | 1.30 m | Wheelchair passage |
| Passing space | 1.80 m (every 15 m) | Two wheelchairs passing |
| Door recesses | ≤ 0.30 m deep | Prevents collision |

#### Internal Doors
| Door Location | Clear Width | Notes |
|---------------|-------------|-------|
| All primary rooms | ≥ 0.76 m | Module 9 minimum |
| Bathroom (accessible) | ≥ 0.80 m | Outward opening preferred |
| Storage/utility | ≥ 0.68 m | Reduced requirement |

**Design Consideration:** Doors should ideally open against walls, not into circulation paths.

### 2.2.4 Accessible Bathroom (BBR 3:146)

At least one bathroom on the entrance level must be fully accessible. This is known as the **"Hygienrum"** or **"RWC"** (rullstolsanpassad WC).

#### Dimensional Requirements

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                         │
│  ┌─────┐                               │
│  │ WC  │     1.30 m turning             │
│  │     │     circle required            │
│  └──┬──┘          ○                    │
│     │         (wheelchair)             │
│  0.80m│                                │
│  side │  ┌─────────────────┐           │
│  space│  │    Basin        │           │
│       │  └─────────────────┘           │
│       │                                │
│       │  1.10 m in front of WC         │
│       │                                │
│  Door opens outward or slides →        │
└─────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

| Requirement | Dimension | Measurement Reference |
|-------------|-----------|----------------------|
| WC side clearance (grab bar side) | ≥ 0.80 m | From WC centerline to wall |
| WC side clearance (other side) | ≥ 0.20 m | From WC edge to wall |
| Front clearance | ≥ 1.10 m | From WC front edge |
| Turning circle | ≥ 1.30 m diameter | Clear floor space |
| Basin height | 0.80 m | Top of basin |
| Mirror | Lower edge at 0.90 m | Seated viewing |
| Grab bars | 0.80 m above floor | Horizontal placement |

#### Fixtures and Fittings
| Item | Requirement | Notes |
|------|-------------|-------|
| WC seat height | 0.45-0.48 m | Above floor (higher than standard) |
| Flush mechanism | ≤ 1.10 m height | Large button/lever |
| TP holder | Reachable from WC | Within arm's reach |
| Shower | Level entry | No step/threshold |
| Drain | Away from shower seat area | Safety consideration |

**Cost Impact:** An accessible bathroom adds 25,000-40,000 SEK vs. standard due to:
- Larger floor area required
- Specialized fittings (wall-hung WC with support frame)
- Reinforced walls for grab bars
- Level-entry shower with linear drain
- Outward-opening or sliding door

### 2.2.5 Kitchen Accessibility (BBR 3:152)

| Requirement | Specification | Purpose |
|-------------|---------------|---------|
| Aisle width | ≥ 1.20 m | Wheelchair maneuverability |
| Counter height | 0.85 m standard | Optional adjustable sections |
| Under-sink clearance | ≥ 0.67 m height | Knee space for wheelchair |
| Appliance placement | ≤ 1.20 m max reach | Upper cabinet accessibility |

### 2.2.6 Bedroom Requirements

At least one bedroom must accommodate:
| Item | Dimensions | Spacing |
|------|------------|---------|
| Double bed | 1.80 × 2.00 m | Standard size |
| Access both sides | 0.80 m each side | Minimum clearance |
| Foot of bed | 1.30 m | Turning space |
| Wardrobe access | 0.80 m in front | Door opening space |

**Minimum Room Width:** 3.20 m (for bed + access + furniture)

## 2.3 BBR Chapter 5: Fire Safety (Brandskydd)

### 2.3.1 Building Classification

Swedish buildings are classified by occupancy and size:

| Class | Description | Examples |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| Br0 | Very high risk | Hospitals, high-rise |
| Br1 | High risk | Apartments > 2 floors |
| Br2 | Normal risk | Multi-family houses |
| **Br3** | **Lower risk** | **Single-family villas** |

**KGVilla Focus:** Class Br3 (Småhus) - single-family detached houses.

### 2.3.2 Fire Cells (Brandceller) - BBR 5:221

A fire cell is a compartment designed to contain fire for a specified period.

#### Villa as Single Fire Cell
A typical villa is considered **one fire cell** with exceptions:

| Adjacent Space | Separation Required | Construction |
|----------------|---------------------|--------------|
| Attached garage | EI 30 + Sa | Fire + smoke seal |
| Boiler room (solid fuel > 60kW) | EI 30 | Fire rated |
| Workshop with flammables | EI 30 | Fire rated |
| Sauna | None (if < 16 m³) | Standard wall |

#### Garage Separation Detail

The wall between living space and attached garage must achieve **EI 30** rating:

```
Living Space                    Garage
    │                             │
    │  13mm Gypsum                │
    │  13mm Gypsum (double)       │
    │  120mm Mineral Wool         │
    │  70mm Steel Studs cc 600    │
    │  15mm Fire-rated Gypsum     │
    │                             │
    └─────────────────────────────┘

Door: EI 30-Sa certified fire door
      (Self-closing, smoke sealed)
```

**Cost Impact:** Fire-rated garage wall adds approximately:
- Double gypsum labor: +3,000 SEK
- Fire door (EI 30-Sa): +8,000-15,000 SEK
- Certification documentation: +500 SEK
- **Total: +11,500-18,500 SEK** vs. standard internal wall

### 2.3.3 Escape Routes (BBR 5:35)

Every sleeping room must have **two independent escape routes**:

#### Route 1: Primary Exit
- Through normal exit doors
- Maximum travel distance: 30 m to outside
- Protected route if through another fire cell

#### Route 2: Emergency Window (Utrymningsfönster)
Windows may serve as emergency exits if they meet:

| Requirement | Minimum | Notes |
|-------------|---------|-------|
| Opening height + width | ≥ 1.50 m total | Combined measurement |
| Minimum width | ≥ 0.50 m | Opening dimension |
| Minimum height | ≥ 0.60 m | Opening dimension |
| Sill height | ≤ 1.20 m | From floor to sill |
| If sill > 1.20 m | Fixed step required | Permanent installation |

**Window Example Calculations:**
```
Example 1: Opening 0.60m × 1.00m
- Total: 1.60m ≥ 1.50m ✓
- Width: 0.60m ≥ 0.50m ✓
- Height: 1.00m ≥ 0.60m ✓
- PASS

Example 2: Opening 0.45m × 1.20m
- Total: 1.65m ≥ 1.50m ✓
- Width: 0.45m < 0.50m ✗
- FAIL (width too narrow)
```

### 2.3.4 Surface Materials (BBR 5:51)

Fire classification of surface materials in residential buildings:

| Location | Wall/Ceiling Class | Floor Class |
|----------|-------------------|-------------|
| Escape routes | B-s1,d0 | Dfl-s1 |
| Kitchen | D-s2,d0 | No requirement |
| Bedrooms | D-s2,d0 | No requirement |
| Bathrooms | D-s2,d0 | No requirement |
| Living areas | D-s2,d0 | No requirement |

**Material Classification Examples:**
| Material | Typical Class | Suitable For |
|----------|---------------|--------------|
| Painted gypsum | A2-s1,d0 | Everywhere |
| Painted wood (thick) | D-s2,d0 | Living areas |
| OSB board | D-s2,d2 | Structure (covered) |
| Parquet flooring | Cfl-s1 | Everywhere |
| Carpet | Varies | Check certification |

### 2.3.5 Roof Safety (BBR 5:5)

#### Ladder/Steps Requirements
| Roof Pitch | Requirement |
|------------|-------------|
| ≤ 6° (10.5%) | None |
| > 6° | Permanent access required |
| > 45° | Steps (not ladder) required |

#### Snow Guards (Snörasskydd)
Required above:
- Entrances
- Walkways
- Play areas
- Neighboring property if close

**Cost for 150 m² villa:**
- Roof ladder: 3,000-6,000 SEK
- Snow guards (full perimeter): 8,000-15,000 SEK

## 2.4 BBR Chapter 6: Hygiene, Health, and Environment

### 2.4.1 Moisture Protection (BBR 6:5)

This is one of the most critical sections for residential construction. Water damage is the **#1 cause of building defects** in Sweden.

#### Wet Room Classification
| Zone | Description | Requirements |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| **Zone 1** | Shower/bath area + 1m perimeter | Full waterproofing (Tätskikt) |
| **Zone 2** | Rest of wet room | Water-resistant substrate |
| **Zone 3** | Adjacent to wet room | Moisture barrier at transition |

#### Floor Slope Requirements
| Area | Slope | Direction |
|------|-------|-----------|
| Shower area | 1:50 to 1:150 | Toward drain |
| General wet room | 1:100 to 1:200 | Toward drain |
| Outside wet room | Away from wet room | Prevent water ingress |

```
Slope Visualization (Cross-section):

Floor Level Outside ─────────────────────────┐
                                              │
           1:100 fall                         │
         ─────────────────────────────────────│ (5mm drop per 500mm)
                                    │         │
                         1:50 fall  │         │
                       ───────────────────────│ (10mm drop per 500mm)
                                  │ │         │
                            ┌─────┴─┴─────┐   │
                            │   Drain     │   │
                            └─────────────┘   │
                                              │
```

#### Waterproofing Systems
| System Type | Description | Cost/m² |
|-------------|-------------|---------|
| Foil system (Folie) | Factory-made membrane | 400-600 SEK |
| Liquid membrane | Painted on, 2 coats minimum | 300-450 SEK |
| Combination | Liquid + reinforcement tape | 350-500 SEK |

**Critical Details:**
- Corner cuffs: Pre-fabricated (mandatory in Zone 1)
- Floor/wall junction: Reinforced tape minimum 100mm each surface
- Penetrations: Sealed sleeves around pipes

### 2.4.2 Water Supply (BBR 6:6)

#### Legionella Prevention
| Location | Temperature Requirement | Purpose |
|----------|------------------------|---------|
| Hot water tank | ≥ 60°C | Kill bacteria |
| At tap | ≥ 50°C (within 30 sec) | Safe delivery |
| Cold water | ≤ 20°C | Prevent growth |

#### Pipe-in-Pipe Systems (Rör-i-Rör)
Required for all concealed water pipes in modern Swedish construction:

```
Structure of Pipe-in-Pipe:

┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
│         Outer Pipe (Protective)      │
│  ┌─────────────────────────────────┐│
│  │    Inner Pipe (Water carrier)   ││
│  │         ↑                       ││
│  │         │ Leakage drains        ││
│  │         ↓                       ││
│  └─────────────────────────────────┘│
│         ↓                           │
│    Drainage to visible cabinet      │
└─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

Benefits:
- Any leak is contained in outer pipe
- Water drains to visible location (distribution cabinet)
- No hidden water damage
- Insurance compliant

### 2.4.3 Radon Protection (BBR 6:23)

Sweden has significant radon from bedrock. Protection is mandatory.

| Radon Level | Classification | Action |
|-------------|----------------|--------|
| < 200 Bq/m³ | Acceptable | Standard construction |
| 200-400 Bq/m³ | Elevated | Remediation recommended |
| > 400 Bq/m³ | High | Mandatory remediation |

**New Construction Target:** < 200 Bq/m³ (mandatory)

#### Radon Protection Methods
| Method | Description | Cost Impact |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| Radon membrane | Polyethylene barrier in slab | +50-100 SEK/m² |
| Sub-slab ventilation | Gravel + extraction pipe | +100-200 SEK/m² |
| Increased ventilation | Mechanical ventilation | System cost |
| Blue concrete check | Identify if used (renovation) | Inspection cost |

## 2.5 BBR Chapter 9: Energy Management

### 2.5.1 Energy Performance Requirements

Sweden divides the country into climate zones:

| Zone | Regions | Primary Energy Limit |
|------|---------|---------------------|
| I (North) | Norrbotten, Västerbotten | 95 kWh/m²·year |
| II (Central) | Most of Sweden | 90 kWh/m²·year |
| III (South) | Skåne, Blekinge, Halland | 85 kWh/m²·year |

### 2.5.2 U-Value Requirements

Thermal transmittance values for building envelope:

| Component | Maximum U-Value | Typical Construction |
|-----------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Roof | 0.08-0.10 W/m²K | 500mm mineral wool |
| External wall | 0.15-0.18 W/m²K | 260mm mineral wool frame |
| Ground floor | 0.10-0.15 W/m²K | 300mm EPS under slab |
| Windows | 0.80-1.0 W/m²K | Triple glazing required |
| Doors | 1.0-1.2 W/m²K | Insulated, sealed |

#### Wall U-Value Calculation Example

```
260mm Energy Wall Calculation:

Layer                    Thickness   λ (W/mK)   R (m²K/W)
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
External surface         -           -          0.04
Facade panel             22mm        0.13       0.17
Air gap (ventilated)     25mm        -          0.09
Wind board               9mm         0.13       0.07
Mineral wool (outer)     45mm        0.036      1.25
Mineral wool (stud)      195mm       0.036      5.42
Stud adjustment          -           -          -0.54
Vapor barrier            -           -          0
OSB                      11mm        0.13       0.08
Gypsum                   13mm        0.25       0.05
Internal surface         -           -          0.13
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
TOTAL R-VALUE:                                  6.76 m²K/W
U-VALUE: 1/6.76 = 0.148 W/m²K ✓ (meets 0.18 requirement)
```

### 2.5.3 Airtightness

| Requirement | Value | Test Method |
|-------------|-------|-------------|
| Max air leakage | 0.6 l/s·m² | At 50 Pa pressure difference |
| Recommended | < 0.3 l/s·m² | For passive house standard |

**Achieving Airtightness:**
- Continuous vapor barrier (taped joints)
- Sealed penetrations (electrical boxes, pipes)
- Proper window/door installation
- Quality control during construction

### 2.5.4 Climate Declaration (NEW 2025)

From July 1, 2025, new buildings require a climate declaration:

**Scope:** Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) for modules A1-A5
- A1: Raw material extraction
- A2: Transport to manufacturer
- A3: Manufacturing
- A4: Transport to site
- A5: Construction

**Limit Values (2025):**
| Building Type | CO2e Limit |
|---------------|------------|
| Small houses (Småhus) | No limit (reporting only) |
| Multi-family | 350-400 kg CO2e/m² (indicative) |

**Cost:** Professional LCA calculation: 15,000-25,000 SEK per project

# 3. PBL - PLAN- OCH BYGGLAGEN

## 3.1 Overview

**Full Name:** Plan- och Bygglagen (2010:900)
**English:** The Planning and Building Act
**Authority:** Boverket (regulations), Municipal Building Committees (enforcement)
**Purpose:** Governs land use planning and building permits in Sweden

### 3.1.1 Fundamental Principles

1. **Local Planning Authority:** Municipalities have planning monopoly
2. **Permit Requirement:** Most construction requires building permit
3. **Supervision:** Construction must be supervised by certified personnel
4. **Completion Verification:** Buildings require final approval before use

---

## 3.2 Building Permit Process (Bygglov)

### 3.2.1 When is Bygglov Required?

| Activity | Permit Required? | Notes |
|----------|------------------|-------|
| New villa construction | Yes | Full application |
| Extension > 15 m² | Yes | Full application |
| Extension ≤ 15 m² (Attefallshus) | No* | Notification required |
| Garage/carport > 50 m² | Yes | Full application |
| Change of use | Often | Depends on change |
| Facade change | Sometimes | Depends on area plan |
| Solar panels | Usually no | Unless heritage area |

*Attefallshus exemption applies to single-family houses only

### 3.2.2 Application Requirements

A complete building permit application includes:

| Document | Description | Who Prepares |
|----------|-------------|--------------|
| Application form | Standard municipal form | Owner |
| Site plan (Situationsplan) | 1:500 showing plot + building | Surveyor |
| Floor plans | 1:100 all floors | Architect |
| Elevations | All facades | Architect |
| Sections | Minimum 1 | Architect |
| Technical description | Building specifications | Engineer |
| Control plan | Quality control procedures | KA |
| KA assignment | Certified inspector contract | Owner |

### 3.2.3 Permit Timeline

| Stage | Typical Duration | Legal Maximum |
|-------|------------------|---------------|
| Application review | 2-4 weeks | - |
| Request for completion | If incomplete | - |
| Decision | 4-10 weeks | 10 weeks |
| Appeal period | 4 weeks | - |
| Total to Startbesked | 8-16 weeks | - |

### 3.2.4 Permit Fees

Municipal fees vary significantly:

| Item | Stockholm | Göteborg | Rural |
|------|-----------|----------|-------|
| Bygglov (150 m² villa) | 40,000-60,000 | 35,000-45,000 | 20,000-30,000 |
| Startbesked | Included | Included | Often separate |
| Slutbesked | Included | 5,000-8,000 | 2,000-5,000 |
| Stakeout (Utsättning) | 5,000-10,000 | 4,000-8,000 | 3,000-6,000 |
| Map extract | 2,000-4,000 | 1,500-3,000 | 1,000-2,000 |

## 3.3 Kontrollansvarig (KA)

### 3.3.1 What is a KA?

The Kontrollansvarig (KA) is a **certified independent inspector** required for most construction projects. They ensure compliance with building regulations.

### 3.3.2 KA Certification Levels

| Level | Scope | Projects |
|-------|-------|----------|
| K (Basic) | Simple structures | Garages, small extensions |
| N (Normal) | Standard buildings | Villas, small apartments |
| A (Advanced) | Complex structures | Large buildings, special designs |

### 3.3.3 KA Responsibilities

1. **Before Construction:**
   - Review technical documentation
   - Assist in preparing control plan
   - Identify critical control points

2. **During Construction:**
   - Site inspections at key stages
   - Verify contractor self-control
   - Document compliance

3. **After Construction:**
   - Final inspection
   - Written statement to municipality
   - Recommend Slutbesked

### 3.3.4 KA Costs

| Project Size | Typical Fee Range |
|--------------|-------------------|
| Small villa (< 120 m²) | 25,000-35,000 SEK |
| Standard villa (120-180 m²) | 30,000-45,000 SEK |
| Large villa (> 180 m²) | 40,000-60,000 SEK |
| Complex design | +20-50% |

## 3.4 Startbesked and Slutbesked

### 3.4.1 Startbesked (Start Notification)

**Definition:** Authorization to begin construction work

**Requirements:**
- Approved Bygglov in force
- Complete control plan
- Appointed KA
- Technical meeting held (if required)
- All pre-conditions met

**Important:** Work started without Startbesked may require demolition!

### 3.4.2 Slutbesked (Final Approval)

**Definition:** Confirmation that building may be occupied/used

**Requirements:**
- All control plan items verified
- KA statement submitted
- Any conditions from Startbesked fulfilled
- Building meets approved plans

**Without Slutbesked:**
- Building may not be occupied
- Insurance may be void
- Fines possible

# 4. SÄKER VATTEN - PLUMBING STANDARDS

## 4.1 Overview

**Full Name:** Säker Vatten 2021:1
**English:** Safe Water
**Organization:** Säker Vatten AB (Industry consortium)
**Status:** Industry standard (not law), but required by insurance companies
**Scope:** Water installations in buildings
**Website:** www.sakervatten.se

### 4.1.1 Legal Status

While Säker Vatten is technically a voluntary industry standard, it is **effectively mandatory** because:
- Insurance companies require compliance for damage coverage
- Most building contracts reference Säker Vatten
- Non-compliance creates liability for installers
- Banks may require compliance for mortgages

- Connect appliances to water
- Work on drainage in wet rooms

## 7.2 Snow Loads

### 7.2.1 Snow Load Zones

Sweden is divided into snow load zones:

| Zone | Characteristic Load (sk) | Typical Regions |
|------|-------------------------|-----------------|
| 1.0 | 1.0 kN/m² | Southern coast |
| 1.5 | 1.5 kN/m² | Central-south |
| 2.0 | 2.0 kN/m² | Central |
| 2.5 | 2.5 kN/m² | Northern lowlands |
| 3.0 | 3.0 kN/m² | Northern highlands |
| 3.5-5.5 | Variable | Mountain areas |

### 7.2.2 Snow Load Calculation

```
Design snow load: s = μi × Ce × Ct × sk

Where:
μi = Shape coefficient (roof geometry)
    - Flat roof (0-5°): 0.8
    - Pitched roof (30°): 0.8
    - Pitched roof (60°): 0
    - Valley: 1.6-2.0

Ce = Exposure coefficient
    - Sheltered: 1.0
    - Normal: 1.0
    - Windswept: 0.8

Ct = Thermal coefficient (usually 1.0)

sk = Characteristic ground snow load (from zone)
```

**Example: Villa in Uppsala (Zone 2.0), 30° pitched roof**
```
s = 0.8 × 1.0 × 1.0 × 2.0 = 1.6 kN/m²
```

| Type | Roof Pitch | Effectiveness |

# 16. RADON PROTECTION

## 16.1 Overview

**What is Radon?**
Radon is a naturally occurring radioactive gas from uranium decay in bedrock. It's colorless, odorless, and the second leading cause of lung cancer after smoking.

**Swedish Context:**
Sweden has significant radon issues due to:
- Uranium-rich bedrock in many regions
- Granite and alum shale deposits
- Historical use of "blue concrete" (blåbetong)

### 16.1.1 Regulatory Framework

| Regulation | Limit | Application |
|------------|-------|-------------|
| BBR 6:23 | 200 Bq/m³ | New buildings (design target) |
| Folkhälsomyndigheten | 200 Bq/m³ | Existing buildings (action level) |
| WHO recommendation | 100 Bq/m³ | Long-term target |

---

## 16.2 Sources of Radon

### 16.2.1 Ground Radon (Markradon)

**Risk Factors:**
| Ground Type | Radon Risk |
|-------------|------------|
| Gravel (clean) | Low |
| Sand | Low-Medium |
| Clay | Low-Medium |
| Granite | Medium-High |
| Alum shale | High |
| Uranium deposits | Very High |

### 16.2.2 Building Materials

**Blue Concrete (Blåbetong):**
- Used 1929-1975 in Sweden
- Contains alum shale with high radium content
- Common in apartment buildings of that era
- Requires special assessment and remediation

### 16.2.3 Water Supply

- Well water from drilled wells can contain radon
- Risk depends on bedrock type
- Water treatment or aeration may be required

---

## 16.3 Protection Methods

### 16.3.1 New Construction

**Standard Protection Package:**
| Method | Description | Cost/m² |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| Radon membrane | PE film under slab, taped joints | 50-100 SEK |
| Sealed penetrations | All pipes through slab sealed | Included |
| Ventilated crawlspace | If applicable | Construction choice |

**Enhanced Protection (High-risk areas):**
| Method | Description | Cost |
|--------|-------------|------|
| Sub-slab depressurization | Gravel layer + extraction pipe | +100-200 SEK/m² |
| Active extraction | Fan + pipe system | +5,000-15,000 SEK |
| Sealed foundation | Extra attention to joints | +50-100 SEK/m |
